# Article Analysis

## Goal

Turn an article into visual anchors, not decoration.

The image should answer: "What does this paragraph help the reader understand or feel that text alone would make slower?"

## Anchor Types

Use these anchor types before writing prompts:

- **Opening detail**: a concrete person, scene, object, commute, message, diagnosis, or unfinished demo that makes the article real.
- **Core tension**: the gap between what people want and what the event/product/workflow can currently deliver.
- **Method insight**: a reusable way of doing things, drawn as a physical action rather than a framework slide.
- **Unfinished problem**: what remains unclear, messy, blocked, or worth returning to.
- **Expectation**: what people ask for next: matching, follow-up, examples, diagnosis, templates, or collaboration.
- **Asset left behind**: cases, leads, question cards, demos, contacts, and next-event themes.

## Shot List Format

Use this concise format:

```text
1. Placement: after section/paragraph ...
   Theme: ...
   Core idea: ...
   Human action: ...
   Object metaphor: ...
   Labels: ... / ... / ...
   Format: 16:9
```

## Xiaohei Method To Preserve

Preserve these habits from 小黑配图:

- Choose the cognitive anchor, not the prettiest paragraph.
- Convert abstract ideas into one physical action.
- Let the character perform the explanation, not point at a diagram.
- Use ordinary objects for complex systems.
- Leave some absurdity so the image is memorable.

## What To Avoid

- Do not illustrate every sentence.
- Do not turn the article into a flowchart.
- Do not make a generic AI/office/business poster.
- Do not make the image only positive if the article contains open questions.
- Do not let labels carry all meaning. The action must do the main work.
